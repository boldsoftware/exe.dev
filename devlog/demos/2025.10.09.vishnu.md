Vishnu was a pre-screen interview for a front-end role (not going to work out).

Among other things, I had him explore and critique the service with me live. Short session.

Website: a lot of the words didn't connect there. "What's a box?" Copy felt a bit scattered/fragmented.

Went straight for `ssh exe.dev`. Clearly not familiar with ssh: he actually stopped and read the host key warning! Managed OK nevertheless. Wasn't scared off by being dropped in a shell. Rather, it sparked curiosity: "ooh, what's this? What can I do with it?" There's a playfulness in discovering a REPL, less humdrum than a website just due to novelty.

Read the ssh hint text, saw we wanted him to run `new`, ran `help` instead. This is actually good, I think: they know how to explore, _and_ they know what they're supposed to do next when they are done exploring. Ran `whoami`, ran `ls`.

He ran the `browser` command. This could be a great onboarding path for GitHub users who don't have cookies set--it lets them skip a subsequent email auth step! But browser might not be the right command name (maybe `web-login`? `set-cookies`?). 📍 Once back on the web he wanted to stay there.

Started a box with Shelley, who made a thing but then he didn't know how to go see it. (Everyone calls Shelley "she", not "it". Do we care? Probably not.) Also, he never saw the welcome site--it's less prominent in the web flow--which is fine but important to remember for educating people along all the paths.

He really wanted an easy backlink from Shelley to exe.dev box management.📍

He wanted a share button pretty immediately. 📍 "What sharing permissions do you want?" "Just private, share this website, and make someone an admin." I think that we should also consider a "share with exe.dev team" for granting us (exe.dev team) debugging permissions

Crazy idea: each person could have their own "root" box that runs the exe.dev REPL, the same was Shelley runs on their boxes. The repl commands could be just plain ol' bash command wrappers that use an API with magic permissions b/c of where it is running. And that could be where the readwrite copy of the readonly mounted-in-all-boxes volume lives. Upgrade/API stability pain, though. It's a thought, anyway, maybe something to return to.

Like everyone else, he ended up magnetically drawn to Shelley, but then not understand the connection between Shelley and everything else...and then felt like Shelley didn't work well for him because he couldn't see the results. Shelley's the only thing you can "do something now" with, which makes it super useful, but also possibly misleading. Means we need to make sure Shelley's toy demos work, and that we educate about "what's next" after Shelley.

And we really need marketing drips for re-engagement.📍

He ran off to a hack-a-thon, and wondered out loud if exe.dev would be useful there. Definitely a marketing channel to explore.

Vishnu followed up later with some suggested website copy: "Start a VM in seconds directly from your terminal. Keep it private, share with an invite link, and get HTTPS with auth proxy out of the box — all for a simple flat rate." 📍 He also suggested an asciinema demo on the main page (or a real xterm button!), like we have discussed.
